Transaction Firewall

Before an agent signs, something has to check what it's signing. Paste anything it's about to act on — a message, a token's metadata, a URL, an address, a signing request — and the firewall flags the patterns behind prompt-injection and malicious signing: instructions that hijack the agent, seed-phrase lures, drain and unlimited-approval language, hidden and look-alike characters, phishing URLs and address poisoning. It runs entirely in your browser — nothing you paste leaves the page.

The firewall is a heuristic pre-sign scan — it flags known attack patterns and explains why, but a clean result is not a guarantee and a flag is not a verdict. Always verify the destination address, the amount and the approval scope yourself. Nothing you paste is sent anywhere; the scan is local. This is a safety aid, not investment advice.
